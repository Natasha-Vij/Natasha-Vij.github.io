Data on hospitalization admissions is one way the scientific community understands how rapidly and vastly COVID-19 spreads. Here is how hospital admissions have occured during the COVID-19 pandemic:





Even though vaccines administration has begun, hospitalizations are still nowhere near as low as they were during the beginning stages of the outbreak. These statistics show the public that COVID-19 is not over even though it might seem like the end of the pandemic is near.

Hospital Patients By Month

This analysis shows that hospitalizations have only increased since the beginning of the pandemic. Besides one slight spike in April 2020, it is clear that hospitalizations started increasing in November 2020. Hospitalizations have seemed to start decreasing since March 2021.

Hospital Admissions vs. Total Cases

Examining the relationship between hospital admissions and total positive cases helps the scientific community understand which nations are seeing high rates of hospitalization. There are four nations that stand out in hospital admissions this analysis: France, Italy, the United Kingdom, and Bulgaria.
