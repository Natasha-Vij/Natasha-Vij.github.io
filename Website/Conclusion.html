In December 2020, vaccine global regulating governing bodies like the U.S. Food and Drug Administration started approving the administration of COVID-19 vaccines. Companies like Pfizer, Moderna, AstraZeneca, Johnson & Johnson were able to quickly manufacture mass doses of their vaccines in order to start administrations.



Top 10 Countries Vaccinating By Manufacturers

As seen in this graph, countries such as the United States, India, and the United Kingdom are leading in terms of total vaccine administration of at least one dose. According to this analysis, top vaccine manufacturers include Pfizer, Moderna, AstraZeneca, Johnson & Johnson and Sinovac. China did not report data for the total vaccinations. However, as China has been a focal point of the COVID-19 pandemic, it is important to note that China is using vaccines manufactured by Sinopharm and Sinovac.

Top 10 Countries in Daily Vaccinations

This analysis indicates that most of the top 10 leading nations in daily vaccine administration are steadily increasing; however, the one country that should stand out is China. China is the only country that seems to not follow a general upward trend in daily vaccinations. There seems to be two major dips in vaccine administration - one from the second week of February - the last week of February and the other from the end of March - mid April (when data was last collected).

Top 10 Countries Reporting Full Vaccine Administration

Since December 2020, there has been a clear push in order to fully vaccinate people globally. As some vaccines require two doses, nations around the world are being pressured to fully vaccinate its citizens as quickly as possible in order to reach an end of this global pandemic. This analysis shows that as of mid-April 2021, over 100 million individuals have been fully vaccinated.

After a year long period of new cases, hospitalizations and deaths, vaccine administration is fully underway for nations around the world. While vaccine manufactures do not believe that vaccines alone will stop the spread of COVID-19, there is global hope that the vaccine will help slow transmission and allow individuals to start living normally once again.
