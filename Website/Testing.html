Top 10 Countries Testing

The United States leads in terms of nations that are leading in testing with close to 400 million tests reported throughout the entire pandemic. India falls second with over 250 million tests, and the United Kingdom follows in third with about 128 thousand cases.

New Tests vs. New Cases

The relationship between total tests conducted and cases confirmed is another way for the healthcare community to observe positivity rates. For this analysis, it is important to take note of any nation that is to the left of the trend line. This trend line helps separate nations that have lower test positivity from higher ones.

Global Tests By Month

Globally, nations reported the most tests during the very end of 2020 and most of 2021. This is most likely due to the virus spreading quickly during this period as well as an ease of accessibility of tests.

Testing helps nations understand how many cases are emerging in any given country. However, tests were not always readily available to the public. During the start of the pandemic, reporting of COVID-19 might not have been as accurate because testing was not always as accessible. Therefore, it is fairly possible that COVID-19 started spreading earlier and faster than actually reported by tests.
