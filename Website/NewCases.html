One way nations report COVID-19 data is by disclosing new case transmission and positivity rates. This data collection is critical in understanding how quickly COVID-19 is spreading.



New Cases By Month

This analysis shows that most continents started seeing cases rise starting in March of 2020. There was a general decrease in the end of summer; however, many continents saw a large spike of cases in mid fall and winter. Europe in particular had the biggest spike out of any continent from September 2020 - November 2020.

Top 10 Average Positive Rate By Country

Positivity rates measure the percent of all COVID-19 tests that are conducted that come out positive. This is a critical measure for the medical community because it gives a baseline level as to what the transmission level is in any given nation.

This analysis looks at average positivity rates from the beginning of the outbreak in February 2020 - April 2021. In this time frame, Mexico and Oman lead in positivity rate, both reaching a total average above 30%.

Top 10 Countries in New Cases

In terms of leading countries in new case transmission, the United States significantly leads with almost 32 million confirmed cases, followed by India and Brazil (both with over 13 million confirmed cases).
